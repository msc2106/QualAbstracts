(a) The Legislature finds and declares as follows: (1) The waters of the state are of limited supply and are subject to ever-increasing demand. (2) Landscapes are essential to the quality of life in California by providing areas for active and passive recreation and as an enhancement to the environment by cleaning air and water, preventing erosion, offering fire protection, and replacing ecosystems lost to development, among other benefits. (3) Landscape design, installation, maintenance, and management can and should be water efficient. (4) Section 2 of Article X of the California Constitution specifies that the right to use water is limited to the amount reasonably required for the beneficial use to be served and that the right does not extend to the waste or unreasonable use of water.
